# Beamstrike Skirmish

**A standalone skirmish wargame for 2 players**

---

## 1. Introduction

Beamstrike Skirmish is a fast-play science fiction skirmish wargame for two players. Each player controls a small warband of 5–15 figures fighting over contested territory, valuable cargo, or survival itself.

Unlike larger wargames, every figure in Beamstrike Skirmish acts individually, activating one at a time in an alternating sequence. The result is a tense, tactical game where positioning, timing, and nerve matter more than raw numbers.

### What You Need

- This rulebook
- 5–15 miniatures per player (any 15mm sci-fi figures)
- A measuring tape or ruler marked in inches
- Several D6 (six-sided dice) and at least two D10 (ten-sided dice) per player
- A playing surface of at least 2' × 2' (a 3' × 3' table is recommended)
- Scatter terrain to provide cover
- Tokens or markers for: Overwatch, Suppressed, Wounds, Loot

### Scale

Beamstrike Skirmish is designed for **15mm miniatures**. Figures typically use 15mm round or square bases. All distances are in inches, calibrated for a 2'–3' table at 15mm scale. Do not rescale distances for larger figure sizes — the game will not balance correctly.

## 2. Warband Creation

### Step 1 — Choose a Race

Both players choose a race for their warband. All races from the Beamstrike universe are available (see Section 11 — Race Special Rules). Your race determines your default Move value, any special rules your figures benefit from, and which alien weapons are available to you.

### Step 2 — Choose Your Figures

A warband consists of three figure types:

| Type | Training | Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Leader** | HERO or ELITE | Exactly 1 (required) |
| **Specialist** | VET or ELITE | 0–3 |
| **Grunt** | REG or CIV | Any remaining |

**Warband size:** Minimum 5, maximum 15 figures total.

**Points limit:** 200 points. All figures, weapons, and equipment must fit within this limit.

### Step 3 — Assign Training

Each figure has a Training class that determines their combat ability and cost:

| Training | Cost | To-Hit Bonus | Melee Bonus | Nerve |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Civilian (CIV) | 0 pts | +0 | −1 | 2 |
| Regular (REG) | 2 pts | +1 | +0 | 3 |
| Veteran (VET) | 5 pts | +2 | +1 | 4 |
| Elite (ELITE) | 10 pts | +3 | +2 | 5 |
| Hero (HERO) | 20 pts | +4 | +3 | 6 |

### Step 4 — Assign Armour

Each figure wears one armour type:

| Armour | Cost | Save |
|---|---|---|
| Unarmoured (UA) | 2 pts | No save |
| Flak Infantry (FI) | 5 pts | 6+ |
| Light Armour (LA) | 8 pts | 5+ |
| Powered Armour (PA) | 18 pts | 4+ |
| Advanced Defence (AD) | 27 pts | 3+ |

### Step 5 — Assign Weapons and Equipment

Beamstrike Skirmish uses the **same weapon list** as the main Beamstrike game. Use the standard weapon reference charts for point costs, to-hit values, and impact types. Vehicle-mounted and Heavy weapons are not available in Skirmish.

**Carry limits:**
- Each figure may carry up to 2 weapons (1 primary + 1 sidearm/melee)
- Support weapons may only be carried by Specialists or the Leader

### Step 6 — Record Your Roster

Write down each figure's name, training, armour, weapons, equipment, Wounds, Nerve, and point cost. Keep this roster throughout a campaign.

## 3. Figure Profiles

Every figure has the following stats:

| Stat | Description |
|---|---|
| **Move** | Maximum distance in inches the figure may move per Move action |
| **Training** | CIV / REG / VET / ELITE / HERO — determines combat bonuses |
| **Armour** | UA / FI / LA / PA / AD — determines damage save |
| **Wounds** | Grunts have 1 wound. Specialists and Leaders have 2 wounds |
| **Nerve** | Value used in Bottle Tests. Set by Training class (see table above) |
| **Skills** | Special abilities the figure has learned (campaign advancement only) |

### Move Values by Race

Standard Move values for each race are listed in Section 11 — Race Special Rules. Human-equivalent races typically Move 4".

### Wounds and Status

**1-wound figures (Grunts):** A single failed armour save puts the figure **Out of Action (OOA)** immediately.

**2-wound figures (Specialists and Leaders):**
- First wound: figure becomes **Suppressed** (see Section 10)
- Second wound: figure goes **Out of Action**

## 4. Core Rules

### Dice

- **2D6:** Roll two D6 dice, add them together. Used for To-Hit rolls. The sum produces a natural bell curve — extreme results (very low or very high) are rare, making mid-range bands the most decisive.
- **D10:** Used for Initiative and Melee. Roll one D10, apply modifiers, compare to the target number. Equal to or higher = success.
- **D6:** Used for the Damage Roll, Injury rolls, Bottle Tests, and Scrip. Roll one D6, apply modifiers.

### Line of Sight

Line of sight (LoS) is determined by drawing an imaginary line from the torso of the firing figure to any part of the target figure's body. Terrain that the line passes through may grant cover (see Cover below) or block LoS entirely.

**Blocking terrain:** Solid walls, buildings, and full-height obstacles block LoS completely. A figure completely behind blocking terrain cannot be targeted.

### Cover

Figures partially obscured by terrain benefit from cover:

| Cover Type | Examples | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Light Cover | Low walls, bushes, cargo crates | −1 to the attacker's To-Hit roll |
| Heavy Cover | Windows, thick pillars, entrenched positions | −2 to the attacker's To-Hit roll |

A figure is in cover if more than half of its body is obscured from the attacker's perspective.

### Measuring

Measure distances from the closest point of one base to the closest point of the other base. You may measure at any time — before or after declaring an action.

### Rounding

Always round up to the nearest whole inch.

## 5. Turn Structure

A game is played over a series of **rounds**. Each round has two steps:

### Step 1 — Initiative

Both players roll 1D10. The player with the higher result wins initiative and chooses who activates first this round. In the event of a tie, the player who lost initiative last round goes first. If it is the first round, both players roll again.

**Leader Bonus:** If your Leader is active (not OOA), add your Leader's Nerve value to your initiative roll.

### Step 2 — Alternating Activation

Starting with the player who won initiative, players alternate activating one figure at a time. Each figure may take **2 actions** from the list below before the next player activates a figure. **Each figure may only be activated once per round.** Interrupt effects (such as Overwatch fire) are not activations and do not count against this limit.

When one player has activated all their remaining figures and the other has not, the remaining player activates all their remaining figures consecutively.

### Action List

| Action | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Move** | Move up to the figure's Move value in inches |
| **Shoot** | Make a shooting attack with a carried ranged weapon |
| **Charge** | Move into melee with an enemy (costs both actions) |
| **Overwatch** | Place an Overwatch marker on this figure (see Section 8) |
| **Stand Up** | Remove Suppressed status from this figure |
| **Reload** | Ready a weapon marked **[Reload]** to fire again |
| **Use Equipment** | Use a carried item (grenade, med-kit, etc.) |
| **Rally** | Roll 1D6 + your To-Hit Bonus; on 6+ remove Suppressed from a friendly figure within 3" |

**Restrictions:**
- A figure may take the same action twice (e.g. Move + Move to cover more ground)
- Charge costs both actions and cannot be combined with any other action
- Overwatch must be the figure's final action and the figure must not have moved this activation
- A Suppressed figure may only take the Stand Up action until it recovers

## 6. Movement

### Standard Move

A figure may move up to its Move value in any direction, including diagonally. Movement may be split: move partway, take another action, then move the remainder — as long as the total distance does not exceed the Move value.

### Terrain

| Terrain Type | Movement Cost |
|---|---|
| Open ground | Normal |
| Rough/difficult ground | Half Move |
| Climbing/ascending | 1" of vertical height costs 1" of Move |
| Jumping down ≤ 3" | Free, no penalty |
| Jumping down > 3" | Figure takes a Falling Hit (see below) |

### Engagement Range

A figure within 1" of an enemy is in **engagement range**. A figure may not voluntarily move out of engagement range unless it first successfully breaks off (requires a Move action; the enemy may immediately make one free Shoot or Melee attack as a reaction before the figure completes the move).

### Falling

If a figure is forced off a height of more than 3", it immediately takes a hit. Roll 1D6 and apply the figure's armour save normally. On a failed save the figure takes a wound as normal.

## 7. Shooting

### Declaring a Shot

To make a shooting attack, the figure must:
1. Have a ranged weapon (if the weapon is marked **[Reload]**, it must have been reloaded since its last shot)
2. Have line of sight to the target
3. Be within the weapon's maximum range

### Range Bands

Beamstrike Skirmish uses the same five range bands as the main game. Measure base to base.

| Band | Distance |
|---|---|
| B1 — Point Blank | 0–4" |
| B2 — Short | 5–20" |
| B3 — Effective | 21–40" |
| B4 — Long | 41–80" |
| B5 — Extreme | 81"+ |

Each weapon has its own **2D6 to-hit threshold per band** — see the weapon profile in the main Beamstrike weapon reference. A band marked **CNF** means the weapon cannot fire at that range. A band marked **—** means the weapon is out of range.

### Making the To-Hit Roll

Roll **2D6** and add the firing figure's **To-Hit Bonus** (from Training). Apply any modifiers below. If the result equals or exceeds the band's threshold, the shot hits. The 2D6 bell curve means mid-range modifiers have the most impact — a single +1 near threshold 8 shifts hit probability far more than the same +1 near an extreme.

**To-Hit Modifiers (applied to the 2D6 roll):**

| Situation | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Target in Light Cover | −1 |
| Target in Heavy Cover | −2 |
| Firer moved this activation | −1 |
| Target is Suppressed | +2 |
| Support weapon at Band 1 (point blank) | −2 |
| Aimed shot (firer spends both actions to shoot the same target) | +2 |

### Damage Roll

When a shot hits, roll **1D6** and calculate the net result:

**Net = 1D6 + Damage Type Bonus + Armour Modifier**

| Net Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| 4 or more | **Out of Action (OOA)** |
| 1–3 | **Suppressed** |
| 0 or less | **No Effect** |

**Damage Type Bonuses:**

| Type | Bonus | Typical Weapons |
|---|---|---|
| Light [L] | +0 | Pistols, knives (ranged) |
| Standard [S] | +1 | Carbines, assault rifles, sniper rifles |
| Heavy [H] | +2 | Support weapons, heavy guns |
| Blast [B] | +2 | Grenades, mortars, rockets |

**Armour Modifiers:**

| Armour | Modifier |
|---|---|
| UA (Unarmoured) | +0 |
| FI (Flak Infantry) | −1 |
| LA (Light Armour) | −2 |
| PA (Powered Armour) | −3 |
| AD (Advanced Defence) | −4 |

*Example: VET fires an Assault Rifle (STANDARD impact → +1) at a PA-armoured figure (−3). Net = D6 + 1 − 3 = D6 − 2. Roll of 6: net = 4 → OOA. Roll of 3: net = 1 → Suppressed. Roll of 1: net = −1 → No Effect.*

### Wound Effects & Wound Stacking

**Grunts (1 wound):** Any Suppressed or OOA result puts the figure Out of Action immediately.

**Specialists and Leaders (2 wounds):**
- **OOA result** → figure goes Out of Action
- **Suppressed result** on a fresh figure → place a Suppressed marker on the figure
- **Suppressed result** on an already-Suppressed figure → place a **wound counter** on the figure (use a die or token)
- **Second wound counter** from any source → figure goes Out of Action

*This wound stacking rule ensures that even weapons that cannot physically roll an OOA result against heavy armour (e.g. a pistol against Advanced Defence) can still accumulate suppression into eventual elimination — it just takes many more shots.*

### Templates and Area Weapons

Some weapons (flame weapons, grenades) use a template or area burst. Place the template as described on the weapon profile. All figures fully under the template are hit automatically; figures partially under the template are hit on a 4+ (roll 1D6).

## 8. Overwatch

### Declaring Overwatch

A figure may go on Overwatch as an action, provided it has not moved this activation. Place an Overwatch marker on the figure. The figure is now watching a chosen arc (the player nominates a general direction — a 180° forward arc is standard).

### Mid-Move Interrupt

When an enemy figure begins a Move action and enters the overwatching figure's line of sight and range at any point during that movement, the move **freezes immediately at that point**. The Overwatch shot resolves before any further movement.

1. The Overwatch figure fires immediately (follow standard shooting rules)
2. Resolve any wound, Suppressed, or OOA results
3. Remove the Overwatch marker from the firing figure regardless of outcome
4. The moving figure (if still active) may now complete the remainder of its movement normally

A figure on Overwatch that has not fired retains its Overwatch marker until the start of its next activation, at which point the marker is removed without firing.

### Limits

- A figure may only be on Overwatch once per round
- Overwatch cannot interrupt a Charge (melee charges resolve fully before any reaction)

## 9. Melee

### Charging

A Charge costs **both** of the activating figure's actions. The figure moves directly toward the target, up to its Move value +2". Rough terrain applies normally during a charge — a figure charging through difficult ground halves its total charge distance (Move +2", then halve). The charge must end with the figure in base contact with the target.

If the charging figure cannot reach the target after moving its full charge distance, the charge fails — the figure moves its full distance toward the target but no combat occurs.

### Resolving Melee

When two figures are in base contact, resolve melee immediately:

1. Both players roll 1D10
2. Each player adds their figure's **Melee Modifier**: Training Melee Bonus + Weapon Melee Bonus (see weapon profile)
3. Compare totals — the higher result wins. **Ties go to the defender.**
4. The loser takes a wound (roll Armour Save as normal)
5. The winner may push the loser back up to 1" if desired

**Multiple attackers:** If a figure is in base contact with more than one enemy, it suffers −1 to its Melee roll per additional attacker beyond the first.

### Melee Bonuses by Training

| Training | Melee Bonus |
|---|---|
| CIV | −1 |
| REG | +0 |
| VET | +1 |
| ELITE | +2 |
| HERO | +3 |

### Breaking from Melee

A figure in engagement range may use a Move action to break away. Before completing the move, the opposing figure immediately makes one free attack (either Shoot at point-blank, or a Melee roll at no penalty). The breaking figure defends normally. If the breaking figure survives, it completes its move.

## 10. Suppressed and Out of Action

### Suppressed

A Suppressed figure:
- Cannot take any action except **Stand Up**
- Cannot benefit from Overwatch
- Is easier to hit (attackers gain +2 to To-Hit rolls against it)

To remove Suppressed: the figure uses the **Stand Up** action on its activation, or a friendly figure within 3" uses the **Rally** action (the Rallying figure rolls 1D6 and adds their own To-Hit Bonus; on a total of 6 or higher, the Suppressed marker is removed).

### Out of Action

A figure that loses its final wound is immediately placed Out of Action. Lay it on its side or remove it from the table. OOA figures take no further part in the game.

In **campaign play**, roll on the Injury Table for each OOA figure after the game (see Section 12).

---

## 11. Morale — Bottle Tests

A warband may be forced to make a **Bottle Test** when morale breaks. There are two triggers, and each trigger is its own separate test:

**Trigger 1 — Leader Down:** When your Leader goes OOA, immediately make a Bottle Test.

**Trigger 2 — Heavy Casualties:** At any point when your active figure count drops to 50% or fewer of your starting figure count, immediately make a Bottle Test.

If both triggers occur simultaneously (e.g. your Leader's death also puts you at 50%), make both tests separately.

### Making the Bottle Test

Roll 1D6 and add the **highest Nerve value** among your remaining active figures. Compare the total to **8**:
- **Total 8 or higher:** Warband holds — continue playing.
- **Total 7 or lower:** Warband bottles out. Your warband immediately withdraws from the field. All remaining figures are considered safe (not OOA) for Injury Table purposes, but you lose the game.

**Note:** If your Leader is OOA, you lose the Leader's Nerve bonus on all subsequent Bottle Tests.

## 12. Race Special Rules

All races from the Beamstrike universe are available for warband selection. Each race has a default Move value and one or more special rules that apply to all figures in the warband.

### Human Factions

Human warbands may draw figures from any ERA faction. All figures use the standard rules with no special modifiers.

| Race | Move | Special Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Human (all ERA factions)** | 4" | None — standard rules apply |

### Alien Races

| Race | Move | Special Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Growwlan** | 5" | Fearsome: enemies within 6" suffer −1 to Bottle Test rolls. Skilled melee fighters: +1 to all Melee rolls |
| **Thuntra** | 4" | Marksmen: +1 to To-Hit rolls at Band 3 and beyond |
| **SPUG** | 4" | Tough: FI armour counts as LA; LA armour counts as PA. PA and AD are unaffected — powered and advanced suits are already at the ceiling of what toughness alone can improve |
| **Ferrapur** | 4" | Grenadiers: may carry one additional grenade beyond normal carry limits; grenades cost 1 pt less |

## 13. Campaign Rules

Beamstrike Skirmish supports ongoing **campaign play**, where warbands develop between games, figures gain experience, suffer injuries, and build a history.

A standard campaign consists of **5 linked games** followed by a **Showdown** — a climactic final battle (use any scenario the players agree on, or the Last Stand scenario with the player who has more total wins choosing sides).

### The Roster

Each player maintains a **Roster Sheet** listing every figure in their warband with:
- Name, Race, Training, Armour, Wounds
- Current XP and Skills earned
- Any Permanent Injuries
- Status (Active / Recovering / Dead)

### Post-Game Sequence

After each game, both players carry out the following steps in order:

#### 1. Determine Winner

Count Victory Points scored during the game (see each scenario). The player with more VP wins. On a tie, the game is a draw.

#### 2. Roll Injuries

For every figure that went OOA during the game, roll 1D6 on the **Injury Table**:

| Roll | Result |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | **Full Recovery** — figure returns next game with no penalty |
| 3 | **Miss Next Game** — figure is recovering; unavailable for the next game only |
| 4–5 | **Permanent Injury** — roll on the Permanent Injury Sub-table (below) |
| 6 | **Death** — figure is removed from the roster permanently |

**Permanent Injury Sub-table (D6):**

| Roll | Injury | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Old Wound | −1" Move (minimum 2") |
| 2 | Arm Injury | −1 to all Shoot To-Hit rolls |
| 3 | Leg Wound | −2" Move (minimum 2"); cannot Charge |
| 4 | Eye Damage | Loses one range band (max range reduced by one band; cannot use Band 5) |
| 5 | Shaken Nerve | −1 to this figure's Nerve value for all Bottle Tests |
| 6 | Battle-Scarred | Survives intact and gains 1 free Skill Slot (immediately choose a skill) |

#### 3. Earn Scrip

Both players earn campaign currency (**scrip**) to spend between games:

- **Base income:** 1D6 scrip (rolled by each player)
- **+1 scrip** per scenario objective achieved
- **+1 scrip** if your warband won the game
- **+1 scrip** if your Leader was not OOA at game end

#### 4. Award XP

Figures earn XP individually for actions during the game:

| Action | XP Earned |
|---|---|
| Taking an enemy Out of Action | 1 XP |
| Completing a scenario objective | 1 XP per figure that participated in completing it |
| Being the last figure standing when enemy bottles | 1 XP (Leader only) |

#### 5. Spend XP — Advancement

Figures may spend accumulated XP to gain skills. Each skill costs **4 XP**. A figure may hold a maximum of **3 skills**.

When a figure earns a skill, choose from the Beamstrike Skirmish Skill List:

**Skills Available:**

| Skill | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Dodge** | Once per game, may re-roll one failed Armour Save |
| **Nerves of Steel** | This figure is immune to the Suppressed result on its first wound |
| **Ambidextrous** | May shoot two sidearms in one Shoot action (resolve both, −1 To-Hit on each) |
| **Constitution** | Gains +1 Wound (maximum 3 wounds total) |
| **Fortune** | Once per game, may re-roll any single dice roll (before the result is applied) |
| **Quick Fire** | May Shoot as a free action after a Move action once per activation |
| **Gun Skill** | +1 To-Hit with all ranged weapons |
| **Aggressive** | +1 to Melee rolls; may re-roll a losing Melee result once per game |
| **Initiative** | Always activates first in the round when initiative is tied |
| **Artillerist** | May use support weapons without the −2 point-blank penalty |
| **Marksman** | Ignores cover penalties at Band 4 and Band 5 |
| **Sharpshooter** | If both actions this activation are Shoot at the same target, +3 To-Hit instead of +2 |
| **Agile** | Ignores difficult terrain movement penalties |
| **Martial Arts** | Unarmed Melee bonus is +2 instead of −1; may attack twice in Melee (roll twice, use higher result) |

#### 6. Spend Scrip

Between games, players may spend scrip on the following:

| Purchase | Cost |
|---|---|
| Recruit a new figure | Equal to the figure's point cost |
| Buy a weapon or equipment item | Equal to its point cost |
| Remove a Miss Next Game status early | 3 scrip |
| Remove a Permanent Injury | 5 scrip (represents surgery, bionics) |

**Warband size limit:** You may not have more than 15 figures available to deploy at the start of any game. Figures currently on "Miss Next Game" do not count toward this limit — their slot may be filled by a recruit. If a recovering figure returns and would push you over 15, you must drop a figure from your roster before the next game (your choice).

## 14. Scenarios

Each scenario lists: **Setup**, **Objectives**, **Special Rules**, and **Victory Conditions**. Before the game, players roll off — the winner picks attacker or defender (where applicable) or chooses table edge.

### Scenario 1 — Last Stand

*Two warbands clash in open territory. No quarter given.*

**Setup:** Players deploy on opposite table edges. Deployment zone: within 6" of your table edge. Both players deploy fully before the game begins (roll off for who deploys first).

**Objectives:** None — destroy the enemy warband.

**Victory Conditions:** The warband that forces the enemy to Bottle Out, or eliminates all enemy figures, wins. Score **1 VP per enemy figure taken OOA**.

**Special Rules:** None. This is the default scenario for learning the rules.

---

### Scenario 2 — Raid

*The attacker must destroy key objectives in the defender's territory. The defender must stop them.*

**Setup:** Defender places 3 Objective markers anywhere in their half of the table, each at least 6" apart. Attacker deploys within 6" of their table edge. Defender deploys within 6" of their table edge.

**Game Length:** 8 rounds, then the game ends.

**Objectives:**
- **Attacker:** Destroy Objective markers. A figure in base contact with a marker may spend both actions to destroy it.
- **Defender:** Protect the markers. Eliminate attackers.

**Victory Conditions:**
- Attacker scores **3 VP per Objective destroyed**
- Defender scores **2 VP per attacking figure taken OOA**
- Defender scores **3 VP per Objective intact at game end**
- Most VP at end of 8 rounds wins. If Attacker forces Bottle Out, Attacker wins immediately.

---

### Scenario 3 — Hold the Line

*Three control zones dispute the center of the battlefield. Hold them longer.*

**Setup:** Place 3 Control Zone markers in a line across the center of the table, evenly spaced. Players deploy within 6" of their respective table edges.

**Game Length:** 8 rounds.

**Controlling a Zone:** A zone is controlled by the player with the most active figures within 3" of the marker at the end of each round. If tied, the zone is contested (no VP scored).

**Victory Conditions:** Score **1 VP per zone controlled at end of each round**. First player to reach **10 VP** wins immediately. Otherwise, most VP after 8 rounds wins.

---

### Scenario 4 — Rescue

*A prisoner is held in enemy territory. Extract them before it's too late.*

**Setup:** The defender places a **Prisoner marker** anywhere in their deployment zone (within 6" of their table edge). Both players deploy within 6" of their respective table edges.

**Prisoner Rules:** The attacker must get a figure into base contact with the Prisoner marker to "free" them. The freed prisoner then moves with the freeing figure (attached) at half Move. The prisoner is extracted when they reach the attacker's table edge. The defender may shoot at the prisoner directly (1-wound, UA — the prisoner is not a warband figure and does not contribute to Bottle Tests).

If the carrying figure goes OOA, place the Prisoner marker at that figure's position. Any attacker figure may move into base contact to pick them up again. Any defender figure may move into base contact to recapture them — return the marker to the defender's deployment zone.

**Victory Conditions:**
- Attacker wins if the prisoner exits via the attacker's table edge, scoring **10 VP**
- Defender wins if the prisoner is killed or the attacker Bottles Out
- Attacker scores **1 VP per defending figure taken OOA** regardless of outcome

---

### Scenario 5 — Ambush

*One force has set a trap. The other must fight through it.*

**Setup:** The **Quarry** (player who lost the roll-off) deploys all their figures within 8" of the center of the table. The **Ambusher** places numbered face-down tokens anywhere on the table outside the center 14" × 14" zone (equal to their figure count + 3 dummy tokens). Tokens are not revealed yet.

**Quarry's Goal:** Move all surviving figures off the Ambusher's table edge.

**Ambush Rules:** When a Quarry figure moves within 6" of a face-down token, flip it. On a figure token: place the corresponding Ambusher figure immediately. On a dummy: remove the token. Ambusher figures act normally from the round they are revealed.

**Game Length:** 10 rounds.

**Victory Conditions:**
- Quarry scores **2 VP per figure that exits via the Ambusher's table edge**
- Ambusher scores **2 VP per Quarry figure taken OOA**
- Most VP after 10 rounds (or when all Quarry figures are OOA or exited) wins.

---

### Scenario 6 — Treasure Hunt

*Valuable cargo is scattered across the battlefield. Take what you can carry.*

**Setup:** Roll 1D6 + 2 to determine the number of **Loot Tokens**. Place them randomly across the center of the table (at least 6" from any table edge and at least 4" apart). Players deploy within 6" of their table edges.

**Carrying Loot:** A figure in base contact with a Loot Token may pick it up using a Use Equipment action. A figure carrying Loot moves at half speed and cannot Charge or go on Overwatch. Loot is dropped if the figure goes OOA (place the token at the figure's position).

**Extracting Loot:** A figure carrying Loot exits the table via their own table edge to score it. Extracted Loot cannot be recovered by the opponent.

**Game Length:** 8 rounds.

**Victory Conditions:** **3 VP per Loot Token extracted off your own table edge**. Most VP at end of 8 rounds wins. If a player Bottles Out, they lose all un-extracted Loot they were carrying.

### Actions Summary

| Action | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Move | 1 action | Up to Move value in inches |
| Shoot | 1 action | Requires LoS and range; [Reload] weapons must be reloaded first |
| Reload | 1 action | Ready a [Reload] weapon to fire again |
| Charge | 2 actions | Move + 2", ends in base contact |

| Rally | 1 action | Friendly within 3"; roll 1D6 + To-Hit Bonus, 6+ succeeds |

### Shooting Summary

1. Check LoS and range band (B1 0–4", B2 5–20", B3 21–40", B4 41–80", B5 81"+)
2. Roll **2D6** + Training To-Hit Bonus ± modifiers vs weapon's band threshold (from weapon profile) = hit
3. Map weapon's Impact to skirmish damage bonus: LOW/STUN=+0 · STANDARD=+1 · HIGH/POWER=+2 · TOTAL=+2
4. Roll **1D6** + damage bonus + Armour Modifier (UA+0, FI−1, LA−2, PA−3, AD−4)
5. Net 4+ = OOA; Net 1–3 = Suppressed; Net 0 or less = No Effect
6. Suppressed result on already-Suppressed figure = wound counter; 2 wound counters = OOA

### Melee Summary

1. Both roll D10 + Melee Bonus (Training + weapon)
2. Higher total wins (ties = defender wins)
3. Loser takes a wound (roll Armour Save)

### Bottle Test Summary

**Triggers:** Leader OOA, or active figures ≤ 50% of start.
**Roll:** 1D6 + highest active figure's Nerve. **8+ = hold.**
